Israeli Cuisine

Whether you are eating a breakfast that is so good that you feel like you are at a five star hotel or just going simple with hummus dip, this cuisine has got it all.

breakfast

Shakshuka

The king's breakfast, Shakshuka is a very famous and known food in Israel. Shakshuka is a rich breakfast of eggs poached in tomato sauce and vegetables. There are many theories from where the dish came. Some say it spread from Ottoman Turkey, while others say from Morroco, or Yemen. The dish was then brought to Israel by Tunishian Jews as part of the exodus from arab and muslim countries where it became part of the local cuisine. The Shakshuka is an amazing food that always continues to surprise.

Hummus

There are many amazing side dishes in Israel that go with every meal, breakfast, lunch, and dinner. The first one on my list is hummus. Hummus is an incredibly delicious dip but what we have in Canada is ten times worse than a traditional Israeli recipe. Hummus was invented in the middle east, most likely in the 13th century in Egypt. It then spread all over the middle east and is eaten in many meals. Hummus goes great with the next food on the list which is the pita.

Pita

As said before, the next item is the pita. In Israeli restaurants complimentary pitas are always served with dips such as hummus just like bread with butter is served here. Pitas originated 14500 years during the stone age in what is now Jordan. The word pita originated from the greek word plakous which means thinner bread or cake.

Falafel

An amazing meat subsitute, falafels are round balls of chickpeas that can be eaten alone or in a pita. Their origin is controversial but most likely from Egypt eaten by the Copts during Lent. In Israel, falafel is known as a fast food and is sold on the street the same way hamburgers are sold here. It is many times sold with Shawarma.
